Trap Control

A comprehensive ball control exercise using multiple heights and distances to perfect trapping technique, body positioning, and immediate play after control.

  • Maintain control on each line
  • Pass back after controlled
  • line 1, low control
  • line 2, mid high control
  • line 2, chest control
  • Right foot up the ladder
  • Left foot down the ladder
  • Shuffle body
  • don't raise foot, keep it low
Why is this important?
  • Works on keeping foot in trap position
  • Works on keeping arms down on higher balls
  • Proper trap technique
  • Shuffling body to ball instead of foot to ball
  • Works on playing the ball immediately after settling
  • Works on being on toes, ready position

Snapback touch

A quick-control technique where the ball is caught and immediately pulled back in a sharp motion, useful for creating space in tight situations.

Rapid Rotation: Throw, Trap & Score

Dynamic three-station drill combining goalkeeping, offensive control, and shooting. Players rotate through goalie and offensive positions, practicing throws, traps, and shots in quick succession. Emphasizes proper trapping technique, purposeful movement, and quick transitions between plays.

    • Goalie Throws, then runs to back of line
    • New Goalie steps in
    • Offense Traps
