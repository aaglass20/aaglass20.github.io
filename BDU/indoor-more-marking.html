Positioning & Off-Ball Runs

Indoor soccer demands constant movement, intelligent positioning, and relentless energy. With limited space and fast-paced action, understanding how to position yourself both offensively and defensively is crucial to dominating the game. Let's press the pace and make every second on the field count!

Our Playing Philosophy: All-Out Attack, All-Out Defense

We play with 6 field players—and here's the key: even the defense plays offense. Everyone contributes to both phases of the game. When we have possession, we attack with numbers. When we lose possession, we defend with intensity.

Offensive Positioning: Press and Attack

Force Them to Play Our Game

When we're on offense, if there's no one to guard, we should be pressing. Push up the field and become an offensive weapon. We want to force the opposing team to play at our pace—fast, relentless, and overwhelming.

Key Offensive Positioning Principles:

  • At least one defender back: Maintain defensive balance with one player staying home
  • Everyone else attacks: The rest of the team pushes into space and creates offensive pressure
  • Press high: Don't sit back—make them uncomfortable with the ball
  • Push into space: Find open areas and stretch the defense
  • Quick transitions: When we win the ball, attack immediately

Defensive Positioning: Mark Up Fast

Everyone Marks, Everyone Covers

Indoor soccer is a fast-paced game, and defense requires immediate reaction. When we lose possession, we all mark up instantly. No spectators—everyone has a job.

Critical Image: This shows how we mark up on defense. Everyone is covered, goal side. We move fast, communicate, and ensure no opponent is left open in dangerous areas.

Defensive Marking Keys:

  • Instant transition: As soon as we lose the ball, find your mark
  • Communication: Call out who you're marking—"I've got #5!"
  • Stay goal-side: Position yourself between your opponent and the goal
  • Quick recovery: Sprint back into defensive shape
  • Cover all threats: No one gets a free look at our goal

Off-Ball Movement: Create Opportunities

Don't Stand Still—Move with Purpose

One of the biggest mistakes players make is staying neutral when they don't have the ball. We need to elevate our play by constantly moving, making runs, and creating passing lanes for our teammates.

Watch: This video demonstrates off-ball movement—making intelligent runs and switches to create opportunities.

Types of Off-Ball Runs to Master:

  • Horizontal runs: Move across the field to drag defenders and open up space
  • Switches: Cross from one side to another to exploit gaps in the defense
  • Checking runs: Come towards the ball to receive a pass
  • Diagonal runs: Cut into space at an angle to receive through balls
  • Overlapping runs: Sprint past a teammate to provide a passing option

Why Off-Ball Movement Matters:

  • Creates space for teammates by pulling defenders out of position
  • Provides passing options for the ball carrier
  • Keeps the defense guessing and disorganized
  • Opens up shooting and scoring opportunities
  • Makes our attack unpredictable and dangerous

Remember: Movement off the ball is just as important as what you do with the ball. Be active, be aware, and make yourself available!

Press the Pace: The 5-Minute Rule

Here's our philosophy: Constantly move for 5 minutes, then sub out. We want to maximize our time on the field by playing at full intensity, then recovering so the next group can do the same.

Why the 5-Minute Rule Works:

  • Maximum effort: Players give 100% knowing they'll get a break
  • High tempo: We maintain relentless pressure on opponents
  • Everyone plays: Regular substitutions keep everyone engaged and fresh
  • Wear them down: Our pace becomes overwhelming for the opposition
  • Build fitness: Short, intense bursts improve conditioning over time

Our Goal

Make it a goal to maximize every second on the field. When you're on, you're going full throttle—pressing, moving, attacking, defending. When you're off, you're recovering and preparing to do it again. This is how we win: with pace, intensity, and constant movement.

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Everyone plays both offense and defense—no passengers
  • ✓ Press high on offense, mark tight on defense
  • ✓ Constant off-ball movement creates opportunities
  • ✓ Use horizontal runs and switches to break down defenses
  • ✓ Play at maximum intensity for 5 minutes, then sub
  • ✓ Indoor soccer is fast-paced—match that tempo and dominate
