Passing Ladder - Group 1

  • Right foot Up
  • Left foot back
  • On toes, light feet
  • Move to ball, don't wait
  • Can be 1 touch
  • Can be 2 touch
  • Coach calls out if 1 or 2 touch
  • Urgent and focus
Why is this important?
  • Works on basic skill
  • Accuracy
  • Move to and away from ball
  • Works both feet
  • Works on being on toes, ready position

  • 1st grid, 2 touch left
  • 2nd grid, 1 touch
  • 3rd Grid 2 touch right
  • 2nd grid, 1 touch
  • 1st grid, 2 touch left
  • Keep head up
  • Coach can hold up a number, get player to call it out while doing
  • move quick
Why is this important?
  • Horizontal movement
  • Use of both feet
  • Processing dif skills in different grids
  • Horizontal movement

Group 3 - Trap Control -Chris

  • Maintain control on each line
  • Pass back after controlled
  • line 1, low control
  • line 2, mid high control
  • line 2, chest control
  • Right foot up the ladder
  • Left foot down the ladder
  • Shuffle body
  • don't raise foot, keep it low
Why is this important?
  • Works on keeping foot in trap position
  • Works on keeping arms down on higher balls
  • Proper trap technique
  • Shuffling body to ball instead of foot to ball
  • Works on playing the ball immediately after settling
  • Works on being on toes, ready position

Good Pass, Play O, Bad Pass Play D - Transitions

  • Goal throws ball to first player
  • Player attempts to pass through gate
  • If pass goes through gate, take off an become an option for leading pass
  • Try to pass before next gate to teamate that should be moving after the pass
  • If Player misses first gate, Second Player becomes offence
  • First player immediatly had to transition from Offense to Defense
  • P2 is tring to score on keeper
  • P1 is trying to defend, not waiting on it
Why is this important?
  • P1 is transition to D to O if they make a good pass
  • P1 is transitioning quickly back to D if they make a bad pass
  • Promotes movement on O side if successful
  • There is a consequence for a bad pass

Pass, Shoot, Defend - Whole Team

  • Short pass forward, Communicate to recipient
  • Call for Drop
  • Rush to dropped ball
  • Quick pass over
  • Top player shoots
  • Defender trys to block
  • After shot, shooter becomes defender
  • Initial passer becomes shooter
  • Defender jumps back in line
Why is this important?
  • Urgency as passer, shooter, defender
  • works on short dump or drop
  • Communications and movement
