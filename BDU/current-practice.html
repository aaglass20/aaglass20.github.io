5 drills to improve your dribbling

A comprehensive diagonal cone series featuring five progressive techniques: right-foot-only dribbling builds dominant side control, left-foot-only strengthens weak foot confidence, alternating feet develops smooth transitions, pull-and-push movements enhance close ball control, and step over moves add advanced footwork. Each variation uses the same diagonal cone setup to systematically improve technical mastery and directional changes.

    Dribble at Home

    • 4 cones - focus on touch on every step
    • Right foot only, inside foot one way, outside foot the other
      • go slow, medium speed, max speed
    • Left foot only, inside foot one way, outside foot the other
      • go slow, medium speed, max speed
    • Left foot only, inside foot one way, outside foot the other
      • go slow, medium speed, max speed
    • Dribble to Left, Left Foot, Pull back Left, Outside Right, continue Right
      • go slow, medium speed, max speed
    • Pull and Push
      • Dribble to Left, Left Foot, Pull back Left, Outside Right, continue Right
      • go slow, medium speed, max speed
    • Same thing, step over

Magic Turn

The Magic Turn is an advanced ball control technique that combines deception with fluid movement. Using quick footwork, players perform a subtle drag-back followed by an explosive spin move, making the ball appear to vanish from one side and reappear on the other. This elegant turn not only bewilders defenders but creates immediate space for the attacking player to exploit. Perfect for tight situations where standard turns are too predictable.

Intro to Juggling

Advance your juggling skills with three specialized techniques: Laced juggling using the top of your foot, Toe juggling for precise control, and Inside juggling for smooth ball manipulation. Each style builds unique control and coordination.
