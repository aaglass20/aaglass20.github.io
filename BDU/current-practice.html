Urgency

You heard me say this word during warmup yesterday. Today we live it.

What does urgency mean to me?

Urgency is playing every moment like it matters - because it does. It's the difference between jogging to a 50/50 ball and getting there first. It's not panic. It's not reckless. It's a decision you make before the ball even moves: I am going to get there first, I am going to act first, I am going to think first.

Where do we need to be urgent?

  • Throw-ins - grab the ball, get it in. Don't let the other team set up.
  • Winning the ball - first to the tackle, first to the loose ball.
  • Losing the ball - the second we lose it, we win it back. First five seconds.
  • Getting open - don't stand and wait. Move, check, show for the pass.
  • Taking a shot - if it's there, hit it. Hesitation kills goals.
  • Transitions - the moment the ball turns over, sprint. Both ways.
  • Honestly? Everything. In soccer, urgency is the baseline.

When are we NOT urgent?

When there is no flow - when the whistle blows, when the ball is dead, when the coach is talking. That's when you breathe. The rest of the time, you go.

Why do we communicate? Why do we stay on our toes?

Both answers are the same: to be urgent. Talking lets your teammate act a half second faster. Staying on your toes lets you move a half second faster. Half seconds win games.

End of practice question

Before we break it down, I'm going to ask you: What is urgent? Have an answer ready.

Practice playing the ball under pressure and delaying the press.

  • Banana Run

    • Patience - look for the mistake
    • Position - make them go right or left
  • Delay offensive attack at all cost
    • Worst thing you can do is dive in
    • Delay to push out of the goal
  • Staggered stance, knees bent, stay on your toes
  • 90% is decision making
