Loop Run Throw-In

A sophisticated throw-in routine that maximizes attacking opportunities through coordinated movement. This set-piece tactic involves three players working in harmony: a defender as the thrower, plus a forward and midfielder executing a designed loop movement pattern. The forward's looping run around the midfielder creates defensive confusion and opens space, while the timed throw down the line exploits the created advantage. This strategic movement not only sets natural picks but also generates dynamic attacking opportunities from what is typically a static situation. The drill emphasizes timing, movement coordination, and tactical awareness to transform throw-ins into attacking opportunities.

  • Defender Throw ball in
  • Forward and Mid Line up next to thrower
  • Forward Loop run around Mid
  • Defender ties run and throws down the line
Why is this important?
  • Attempt to pick defender
  • Cause Movement

Criss-Cross Throw-In

An effective throw-in routine that creates space through synchronized crossing movements. This set-piece combination features a defender as thrower working with a forward and midfielder who execute precise crossing runs. The coordinated criss-cross movement pattern creates confusion for defenders while opening passing lanes. Success depends on the thrower's ability to read the developing situation and time the throw perfectly to find the freed player. This tactical setup transforms a standard throw-in into a potential attacking advantage by using deliberate movement to create space and opportunities.

  • Defender Throw ball in
  • Forward and Mid Line up next to thrower
  • Forward and Mid Criss Cross
  • Defender looks for open run, times run
Why is this important?
  • Cause Movement to free player

Direct Throw-In Technique

A fundamental approach to throw-ins that emphasizes direct, efficient ball delivery without complex movements. This technique focuses on straight-line throws and proper positioning, eliminating unnecessary looping runs or elaborate setups. By maintaining simple, direct throws and inline positioning, teams can maintain possession more effectively while reducing the risk of turnovers. This straightforward approach is particularly valuable in defensive situations or when quick, secure ball distribution is needed.

Throw-In Control & Touch Drill

A comprehensive throw-in drill that develops essential receiving and control techniques using a three-cone setup. Players practice precise throw-ins and various receiving methods including different foot touches and turning movements. The drill incorporates checking runs and return passes, building from basic control to more advanced variations. This progressive training sequence helps players master fundamental throw-in reception skills while introducing tactical elements through movement patterns and different touch variations. The structured approach allows players to build confidence with basic techniques before advancing to more complex control methods.

Triple Threat Throw

Three attackers set up similar to corner formation: first player breaks near, second attacks center, third drifts wide. Utilizes long throw specialist to deliver ball with corner-kick power into dangerous areas.
