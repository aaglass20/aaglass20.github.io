Delay And Chase Down

1v1 with a covering defender. The primary defender delays the attack long enough for the second defender to step in and win the ball — patience, positioning, and team defending over reckless tackles.

Setup
  • Offense and defense line up across from each other
  • A second defender starts diagonally behind the primary defender
  • Primary defender approaches the attacker — the goal is to delay, not immediately steal
  • Slow the play down long enough for the supporting defender to step in and win possession
  • Staying in front of the attacker creates chances for both defenders — pressure, control, and teamwork force mistakes
  • Once the attacker makes an error, the delaying defender can also look to win the ball
Variations
  • Approach work — close down quickly with intensity, but learn when to slow down and stop at the correct defensive distance so you don't get beaten. Builds balance, body control, and proper spacing.
  • Midfield-line containment — primary defender delays and contains, preventing the attacker from crossing the midfield line. Teaches angling attackers away from dangerous space.
Goals
  • Offense: 1v1 moves, ball control, find a way to score
  • Defense: patience, communication, trust, proper defensive angles, defend as a unit rather than relying on individual tackles

Shooter Vs Defense

Fun drill if we have time, make it a competition

  • 5 balls lined up at 18 yard line
  • Striker on ones side, Defender on other
  • Defense is only allowed to shuffle and get body in front to stop strike
  • Sticker runs back and forth and only shoots when finding and opening
  • D is only trying to block
Why is this important?
  • Works on body placement for D
  • Urgency on closing in for D
  • Works on quick shot
