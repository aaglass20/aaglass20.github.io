Shooting

Rapid Release: Quick Touch & Shot

A dynamic finishing drill where players work around stationary poles that simulate defensive pressure. The exercise begins with a player positioned between poles, receiving a pass before executing a quick touch to create space for a shot. The poles serve as reference points for tight control and realistic defender positioning, forcing players to utilize sharp touches and rapid body rotation to find shooting angles. This drill develops essential striking skills including quick ball control in tight spaces, explosive turning movements, and efficient shot release under simulated defensive pressure. Perfect for enhancing close-quarter ball manipulation, spatial awareness, and clinical finishing in game-like scenarios.

Striking Windows: Shot Selection Challenge

A focused 1v1 shooting exercise set at the 18-yard line with five prepared balls. The drill pits an attacking player against a defensive player in a tactical shooting scenario. The striker moves laterally, seeking optimal shooting opportunities while the defender is restricted to shuffling movements and body positioning to block shots. This specialized format forces attackers to recognize and exploit shooting windows quickly, while defenders must master efficient lateral movement and body positioning. The drill enhances shooting decision-making, defensive positioning, and shot blocking techniques under controlled competitive conditions. By limiting defensive actions to blocking only, the exercise creates a focused environment for both roles to develop their specific technical skills.

  • 5 balls lined up at 18 yard line
  • Striker on ones side, Defender on other
  • Defense is only allowed to shuffle and get body in front to stop strike
  • Sticker runs back and forth and only shoots when finding and opening
  • D is only trying to block
Why is this important?
  • Works on body placement for D
  • Urgency on closing in for D
  • Works on quick shot

One-Touch Finish: Dynamic Strike Drill

A high-tempo finishing drill focused on first-time shooting accuracy. Players make coordinated runs across the box while receiving passes from a coach or fellow player, executing immediate one-touch finishes. The drill emphasizes precise timing, clean ball striking, and accurate placement while in motion. Multiple variations enrich the exercise: incorporating player-to-player passing, diagonal crash runs, or multiple attacking runners. This versatile drill develops crucial match skills including shot timing, movement coordination, and clinical finishing under game-like conditions. The progressive variations allow coaches to adjust complexity and match specific tactical scenarios while maintaining focus on quick release shooting technique.

  • Coach passes ball to moving player
  • Player 1 touch shot
  • Move line with pace
  • Variation would be player passing instead of coach
  • Variation could be crashing instead of running across
  • Variation could be more the one player crash
Why is this important?
  • Focus on quick strike
  • Focus on placement

Complete Striker Series: 4-Phase Finishing

An intensive striking sequence that develops four distinct finishing scenarios in one fluid drill. Working against a live goalkeeper, strikers progress through a series of defined actions: a central dribble and shoot, attacking cuts from both flanks using alternate feet, and culminating in a sophisticated combination play with an aerial finish. Each phase challenges different aspects of striking technique - from direct dribbling attacks to sharp cutting movements and technical combination play. This comprehensive drill sharpens diverse finishing skills while providing goalkeepers valuable shot-stopping practice against varied attacking approaches. The progression builds technical versatility and tactical awareness, preparing strikers for the range of finishing opportunities they'll encounter in match situations.

    • Striker and goalie practice
    • Striker receives 4 balls
    • Ball 1: Shoot on dribble after center cone
    • Ball 2: Run to outside of cone, cut in & Left Foot shoot.
    • Ball 3: Run to other outside of cone, cut in & Right Foot shoot.
    • Ball 4: Run to other center cone, receive and play back, receive in air ball, finish
    Why is this important?
    • Works on 4 shot types
    • Movement
    • Work on placement
    • Works goalie

Quick Release: Touch & Strike Drill

A dynamic finishing exercise focusing on explosive first-touch control and rapid decision-making. Players receive a pass and must immediately transition into an attacking movement, culminating in either a quick shot or a tactical pass. When passing is chosen, the drill extends into a one-touch finishing opportunity, demanding constant alertness and technical readiness. This drill emphasizes the critical transition from reception to attack, developing players' ability to exploit small windows of opportunity. The exercise builds essential striking skills including quick ball control, decisive dribbling, and efficient shot release, while also incorporating combination play options. Perfect for developing the split-second decision-making and technical execution required in match situations.

    • Striker receives ball
    • Quick reaction from first touch to dribble
    • Quick shot or pass
    • If pass, works on quick reaction to 1 tiner
    Why is this important?
    • Learn to move off quick touch
    • Learn to release quick after dribble

Post up, Flip, Hit

A targeted striking drill that simulates creating shooting opportunities from tight marking situations. Players begin by receiving the ball with their back to goal, establishing a strong posting position against defensive pressure. The key focus is on the explosive transition from a shielding position to a striking position through a quick turn. This drill develops essential skills for forwards who need to operate in crowded defensive areas - teaching them how to use body position to protect the ball, create separation through a sharp turn, and execute a quick shot before defenders can recover. The exercise emphasizes the crucial ability to manufacture shooting opportunities even when initially marked out of a favorable position.

    • Receive ball from coach
    • Post on defender
    • Quick turn
    • Quick Shot
    Why is this important?
    • Works on not being open to creating space for quick shot

Intro to Volley

Step-by-step introduction to basic volley technique. Start with simple height and movement drills, progressing to controlled contact. Perfect for first-time learners building confidence with balls in the air.

Cross to Volley or 1 timer

Three attackers time their runs as winger prepares cross: first crashes near post, second attacks center, third drifts back post. Each ready for one-touch finish or volley. Timing and spacing critical for avoiding defenders.

Want Better 1 Touch Finishes

A solo finishing drill where the player serves the ball to themselves while running along a half-circle path, finishing with a one-touch shot on goal. This drill develops timing, curved running patterns, and first-time finishing ability while simulating game-like shooting angles.

Liverpool Finishing Drill

A dynamic two-player finishing drill combining a bounce pass, well-timed through ball, and one-touch finish. The first player executes a bounce pass to their partner, who plays a through ball into space, allowing the original player to complete the sequence with a first-time shot on goal.

Proper Plant and Shot

Demonstrates the critical differences between effective and ineffective plant foot positioning when shooting. Proper technique places the plant foot alongside the ball with correct distance and angle, enabling power and accuracy. Poor positioning, whether too close, far, or misaligned, reduces shot quality and highlights why proper foot placement is fundamental to consistent shooting.
