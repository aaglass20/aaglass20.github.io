Finishing On Target — April 14, 2026

Session Overview

Focus: Finishing on target — low shots, placement over power

Age Group: U12  |  Duration: 60 min  |  Players: 12

Context: Last game: 1 goal scored, multiple shots over the bar and wide. Primary issue is body shape at contact — players leaning back and swiping across the ball.

Objectives

  • Strike through the center or lower half of the ball with the plant foot beside — not behind — the ball
  • Pick a corner and aim for it before the shot, not during
  • Release the shot when the opportunity is there — don't wait for a "perfect" moment inside the box

Session Theme

"Low and on frame beats high and hard. Every time."
A goalkeeper can't stop what they can't reach. A shot on target gives you a chance. A shot over the bar gives you nothing. Today every rep has one standard: it must be on frame.

Warm-Up (10 min)

Dynamic Movement (5 min)

  • 1, 2, 3, Touch Toe → 1, 2, 3, Blue Devils
  • Lunges → Blue, Devils, Blue, Devils
  • Hug Knee to Chest → Blue, Devils, Blue, Devils
  • Cradle Leg and Ankle → Blue, Devils, Blue, Devils
  • Criss Cross Quad → Blue, Devils, Blue, Devils
  • Skips
  • Stand in Place (10 Counts each): Scissors, Front to Back Hop, Side to Side, Criss Cross, Hops
  • Sprint

Ball Work — "Contact Point" Passing (5 min)

Setup: Pairs, 8 yards apart, one ball each pair. 12 players = 6 pairs.

  • Round 1: Inside-of-foot passes — focus on planting the non-kicking foot beside the ball. Say it out loud: "plant beside."
  • Round 2: Laces passes — lean forward, head over ball, strike through the center. Ball must stay below knee height of receiver.
  • Round 3: One-two combos — player passes, receives back, passes again. Keep moving feet.

Coaching cue: "If your plant foot is behind the ball, your body leans back and the ball goes over. Plant beside. Head over. Ball stays down."

Technical: Corner Targeting (15 min)

Setup

  • Full-size goal (or 2 smaller goals if full-size not available)
  • Cones marking 4 spots: two at 14 yards out (slightly left and right of center), two at 10 yards out (slightly inside the posts)
  • 6 balls at each shooting station, split into 2 lines of 6 players each
  • Coach or GK in goal

Progression 1 — Static Ball, Technique Focus (5 min)

Ball placed on cone. Player calls a corner ("near post low" or "far post low") before approaching. Then shoots.

  • Plant foot 6–8 inches beside the ball, toe pointing at the target
  • Lean forward — chest over the knee, knee over the ball
  • Strike with laces through the center or lower half of the ball
  • Follow through low — kicking foot finishes pointing at the target, not at the sky

Counting rule: Only count shots that land below crossbar height. Say "good" out loud when it's on frame. No comment when it goes over — just reset.

Progression 2 — Serve to Self (5 min)

Player stands 3 yards behind the ball. Tosses it forward with their hands, takes 1 touch to set, then shoots. Same targeting rule: call the corner before you shoot.

  • This simulates receiving a pass in stride — set your feet, then strike
  • Common error to watch: players rush the touch and lean back to "get more power" — freeze and correct immediately

Progression 3 — Pass and Shoot (5 min)

Player receives a pass from a feeder standing 6 yards to the side, takes 1 touch to set the ball, shoots. Feeder rotates into the shooting line after each pass.

  • First touch must be forward and out of feet — not directly underfoot
  • Shoot within 2 seconds of the first touch
  • Call the corner before the ball arrives

Key Coaching Points

  • Plant foot beside, not behind — behind = lean back = ball over
  • Head over the ball at contact — eyes on ball until it's gone
  • Strike the lower half of the ball — this is what keeps it down
  • Decide before you shoot — pick a corner, then commit

Tactical: 2v1 Finishing Waves (15 min)

Setup

  • Full-size goal with GK (rotate a field player into goal every 3–4 minutes)
  • One defender starts at the top of the 18-yard box
  • Two attackers start at the halfway line with a ball
  • Coach stands at the side with extra balls to feed the next pair immediately after each rep
  • 12 players: 2 attacking, 1 defending, 1 in goal, 8 waiting. Rotate quickly.

Rules

  • Attackers must get a shot off within 8 seconds of crossing the 18-yard line
  • Goal only counts if the ball stays below the crossbar
  • Defender can only engage once the attackers cross the cone line at 22 yards
  • After scoring or ball goes dead, next pair goes immediately — keep the reps flowing

Coaching Points

  • Shooter's first job is to get on frame — take the chance when the defender commits to one player
  • Non-shooter: create the angle — drag the defender wide so the shooter has a lane
  • Near post first — aim for the near post low; GK has to move. Far post is a bonus.
  • Don't square the ball for a tap-in unless you're 3 yards out — at 12 yards, just shoot

Common Errors to Watch

  • Over-dribbling: players carrying the ball into traffic instead of releasing early — stop play, reset, ask "when did you have the shot?"
  • Rushed shot with no plant foot — defender pressure causes the lean-back reflex — remind them to take that extra 0.5 seconds to set their foot
  • Passing to a covered teammate when the shot is open — freeze and ask the group "what would you do here?"

Small-Sided Game: Low Shot Rule 6v6 (15 min)

Format

  • 6v6 (5 field players + GK each side) on a 40 x 30 yard field
  • Two full-size goals (or 7v7 goals)
  • 12 players, no subs — everyone plays the full game

The Condition

Goals only count if the ball is below waist height when it crosses the line. Shots above the waist = goal kick, no goal. Coach is the judge — call it clearly and quickly so the game doesn't stop long.

Bonus Rule (activate at 8 min mark)

Any goal scored with one touch inside the 18 counts as 2 points. This rewards quick shooting over holding and dribbling in the box.

How to Coach It Live

  • Let the game run — don't freeze play for every missed shot
  • When 3 shots in a row go over, call a 10-second pause: "What are we forgetting?" Let a player answer. Then restart.
  • Shout positive reinforcement when a low shot goes in: "Yes — that's exactly what we worked on."
  • Watch for the player who is scared to shoot — encourage them specifically: "If it's there, trust it and hit it."

Keep Score

Competition matters at U12. Keep the score, let them care about it. The condition is the coaching — you don't need to stop constantly.

Debrief (5 min)

Cool-Down

  • Walk one slow lap around the field
  • Sit in a circle: quad stretch (30 sec each leg), hamstring stretch (30 sec each leg), hip flexor lunge stretch (30 sec each side)

Questions for the Group

  1. "What's the one thing you focused on before every shot today?" — looking for: plant foot placement, head over ball, picking a corner. Let 2–3 players answer.
  2. "In our last game, why did shots go over the bar?" — looking for: leaning back, rushing, plant foot behind the ball. Don't answer it for them.
  3. "Next game — someone puts you through on goal, one-on-one with the keeper. What do you do?" — looking for: stay calm, pick a corner, keep it low.

Connection Forward

"We scored 1 goal last game. We had more chances than that. The difference between 1 goal and 4 goals isn't shooting harder — it's shooting smarter. Plant foot beside the ball. Head over. Pick your corner. If you remember those three things, you'll finish your chances."

Next session can build on this with finishing under pressure — add a closing defender from behind to simulate real game speed.
