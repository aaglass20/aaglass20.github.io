Scanning

Why are we scanning?

  • Awareness
  • Engagement
  • Planning your Next move

Summary on what we are scanning for:

  • Ball
  • Opposition
  • Teammates
  • Space

Scanning Exercises

Scanning for Color

  • Player 1 (or coach) holds two cones, each in a different color.
  • Player 1 raises one cone to indicate the color.
  • Player 2 identifies and calls out the color.
  • Player 1 passes the ball to Player 2.
  • Player 2 controls the ball and returns the pass to Player 1.

Scanning for Direction

  • Player 1 (or coach) holds two cones, each in a different color.
  • Player 2 identifies and calls out the color of the raised cone.
  • When Player 1 raises one hand, Player 2 moves in the opposite direction.
  • The raised hand is used to simulate the side of the pressure.

Know your move before you move - scan

  • Never stop or keep the ball in neutral
  • Move with a direction and be confident
  • Adjust
  • Read the pressure

Protect the Egg

Works on Shielding, physical, grit

  • Make a small square with cones
  • On player stands on ball and keeps away from other player
  • Other Player physically and legally attempts to steal the ball
  • Player with ball is goal is to stay in middle of square

Interception Drill

Simulates reading or anticipating a pass to intercept

  • Two sides to this drill, teaching anticipation
  • Second is scanning
  • Shield the player from intercepting
  • Interceptor fights off the shield
