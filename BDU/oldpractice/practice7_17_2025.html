5 drills to improve your dribbling

A comprehensive diagonal cone series featuring five progressive techniques: right-foot-only dribbling builds dominant side control, left-foot-only strengthens weak foot confidence, alternating feet develops smooth transitions, pull-and-push movements enhance close ball control, and step over moves add advanced footwork. Each variation uses the same diagonal cone setup to systematically improve technical mastery and directional changes.

    Dribble at Home

    • 4 cones - focus on touch on every step
    • Right foot only, inside foot one way, outside foot the other

    • Left foot only, inside foot one way, outside foot the other
      • go slow, medium speed, max speed
    • Left foot only, inside foot one way, outside foot the other

Three Types of First Touch

Master three essential first touches on aerial balls: cushion with the sole of your foot for close control, use the instep for directional control, and strike with your laces to redirect the ball with power.

Reaction Finishing

A quick-thinking finishing drill focused on reaction speed and first-time shooting. Players face the goal while a coach stands behind them, dropping balls over their head at varying heights and speeds. The striker must quickly locate the ball and attempt to score with either a first-time shot or within two bounces. This exercise develops critical striking skills including awareness, quick decision-making, and adaptable finishing technique under time pressure. Ideal for improving spontaneous shooting scenarios commonly encountered during matches.

Liverpool Finishing Drill

A dynamic two-player finishing drill combining a bounce pass, well-timed through ball, and one-touch finish. The first player executes a bounce pass to their partner, who plays a through ball into space, allowing the original player to complete the sequence with a first-time shot on goal.

Want Better 1 Touch Finishes

A solo finishing drill where the player serves the ball to themselves while running along a half-circle path, finishing with a one-touch shot on goal. This drill develops timing, curved running patterns, and first-time finishing ability while simulating game-like shooting angles.
