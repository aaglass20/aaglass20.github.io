Day 2: 1v1 Defending

Individual defending techniques and winning duels

Drill 1: Jockeying Practice

10 minutes Pairs Beginner

Attacker dribbles slowly, defender jockeys and guides them toward the sideline. No tackling - focus on body position and patience.

  • Stay on your feet - don't dive in
  • Guide attacker toward touchline
  • Keep proper distance (arm's length)
  • Watch the ball, not the attacker's hips

Drill 2: 1v1 Channel Game

15 minutes Pairs Intermediate

Set up a narrow channel (5-8 yards wide). Attacker tries to beat defender and dribble to end line. Defender must stay in channel and win the ball.

  • Use channel edges to your advantage
  • Time your challenge when attacker's touch is heavy
  • Stay low and balanced when tackling
  • Win the ball, don't just kick it away

Drill 3: Tackle Timing

15 minutes Pairs Intermediate

Practice block tackles and poke tackles. Attacker runs at defender, defender must time the tackle perfectly when the ball is exposed.

  • Wait for the right moment - ball away from attacker's foot
  • Commit fully when you go for the tackle
  • Use inside of foot for block tackle
  • Poke tackle for longer reach situations

Drill 4: 1v1 to Goal

20 minutes Pairs + GK Advanced

Full 1v1 situation. Attacker starts 25 yards from goal, defender closes down. Attacker tries to score, defender must stop them legally.

  • Close down quickly while ball travels
  • Show attacker away from goal (toward weak foot if possible)
  • Delay if you can't win it - wait for help
  • Block the shot if attacker shoots

Key Coaching Points

  • Patience Wins: Most goals come from defenders diving in too early
  • Timing: Strike when the ball is furthest from attacker's foot
  • Body Position: Stay side-on, force them to their weak side
  • Physicality: Be strong but fair - use your body legally
  • Recovery: If beaten, don't give up - sprint to recover
