Goalie Drills

30-45 Minute Goalie Training Routine

Warm-up (10 minutes)

  • Set Position Practice (2 min) - Focus on proper feet and shoulder alignment - Practice ready position on balls of feet
  • Arc Movement Practice (3 min) - Practice proper positioning relative to goal - Use cones to mark proper arc movement paths

  • - Dive Activation (5 min)

    - Complete the 5-step dive activation progression - Both sides, focusing on proper form

Core Training (25 minutes)

  • Angle Training (10 min) - "6-18" positioning drill - Triangle positioning practice - Angle bisector exercises
  • Diving Progression (15 min) - Collapse, dive, and step drills - Reaction diving exercises - Scoop and cover practice

Cool Down (5-10 minutes)

  • Distribution Practice - Basic throwing technique - Rolling practice for accuracy
  • Communication Drills - Practice key commands: "Keeper!", "Away!", "Step!"

Key Focus Points:

  • Maintain proper set position throughout all drills
  • Stay alert and on balls of feet
  • Keep shoulders and feet aligned with the ball
  • Use constant communication during exercises
  • Focus on proper technique over speed

Communication

Goalkeepers must be vocal leaders. Use short, clear, confident commands:

  • “Keeper!” – When claiming the ball
  • “Away!” – Clear the ball
  • “Step!” – Push the defensive line up
  • “Drop!” – Fall back into position
  • “Man on!” – Teammate is under pressure
  • “Mark up!” – Assign defenders to attackers

Be loud, confident, and decisive—your voice prevents confusion and mistakes.

Diving Drills

Develop quick reflexes and lateral movement with diving drills. Focus on:

  • Powerful push-offs with your legs
  • Leading with your hands to secure the ball
  • Landing safely to avoid injury

Throwing

Use an overarm throw for quick counterattacks, or a sidearm roll for precise distribution to nearby teammates.

Punting

For long clearances, drop the ball at waist height and strike with the laces. Consider wind conditions and the position of teammates.
