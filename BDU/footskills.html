Foot Skills

5 Beginner Footwork Drills To Master

Want quick feet? Want to improve your footwork skills? This is the perfect drill set to focus on.

Ball Steps

A fundamental drill focusing on rhythmic ball control using alternating steps. Players develop basic touch, timing, and coordination while maintaining a consistent tempo over the ball.

Quick Feet

A high-intensity footwork exercise focusing on rapid touches and quick movements to enhance agility and ball control at speed.

Drag Back in Inside Out<

A technical drill combining a drag back move with inside-out touches to practice ball manipulation and direction changes for effective dribbling control.

Inside Out

A footwork exercise focusing on alternating between inside and outside touches of both feet to improve close ball control and movement flexibility.

Ball Steps

A foundational exercise where players practice quick stepping motions around and over the ball to develop coordination and basic ball awareness.

Maestro

An advanced ball mastery exercise combining quick touches, rolls, and direction changes to enhance overall ball control and dribbling finesse.

Pull Push

A fundamental skill drill that practices pulling the ball back and pushing it forward in a controlled manner to develop touch control and change of direction.

Pull Trap

A control technique that combines pulling the ball back while trapping it under the foot, useful for receiving passes and creating space in tight situations.

Quick Feet Rollovers

An agility drill combining quick footwork with ball rolling movements to improve coordination and close ball control while maintaining speed.

Quick Feet Snap Rollovers

An advanced variation of rollovers incorporating sharp, snapping motions to enhance ball manipulation skills and quick direction changes.

Scissors

A classic dribbling move where players step around the ball in a scissors-like motion to deceive defenders and change direction effectively.

Sole Rolls

A fundamental control exercise using the sole of the foot to roll the ball, building comfort and control in ball manipulation.

Step overs

A deceptive skill move where players step over the ball without touching it, used to unbalance defenders and create space.

Step Unders

A technical drill focusing on quick foot movements under the ball to develop close control and improve ball awareness.

The Vee

A control technique where the ball is moved in a V-shaped pattern using the inside of both feet to change direction quickly.

The Vee II

An advanced variation of the Vee move incorporating additional touches and direction changes for enhanced ball mastery.

Three Tap Spin

A dynamic skill combining three quick touches with a spinning motion to maintain ball control while changing body orientation.

Triangles

A passing pattern drill where the ball moves in triangular shapes to develop consistent touch and directional control.

Wall - Inside Right and Left

A wall training exercise focusing on inside foot passing technique using both right and left feet individually.

Wall - Inside Alternating

A wall drill that practices alternating between right and left inside foot passes to develop bilateral control.

Wall - Laces

A wall training technique using the laces (top of foot) to improve striking power and accuracy with direct kicks.

Wall - Both Fee

A comprehensive wall training exercise incorporating both feet to develop balanced control and passing ability.

Wall - Triangle

An advanced wall training drill creating triangular passing patterns to improve touch, movement, and spatial awareness.
