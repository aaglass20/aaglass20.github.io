Taking Chances

We talked with the team about taking chances—giving yourself the opportunity to make mistakes, learn, and succeed. Here’s what we encourage:

Key Reminders

  • Remember 50 touches: We Always take a touch before passing.
  • Quick touch: Get in the mindset of taking one quick touch.
  • Look up: Be aware of the field and what's next.
  • Take the next action:
    • If you can dribble, then dribble.
    • If you can pass, then pass.
    • If you can shoot, then shoot.
  • While dribbling: Keep peeking up—assess if you’re in danger.
    • If you’re at risk, pass or shoot just before the danger becomes too close.

When Passing

  • Communicate: Let others know where the ball is going.
  • Move after the pass: Position yourself better after each pass.

When Shooting

  • Anticipate a follow-up: Be ready for the next play.
  • Defensive transition: Prepare to switch to defense immediately.

Marking

We refer to the goalie box as the danger zone. In this danger zone, we should be so close to our player that we can reach out and grab their shirt.

On the indoor field, our half is practically the entire danger zone. We need to be in a constant mindset of staying within "shirt-grabbing" distance, ensuring that we are fully marked up in our defensive half. If the opposing team plays a defender up, it gives us a slight advantage; our top-position players can help while still keeping a vigilant eye on their defensive responsibilities.

Takeaways:

  • Danger zone: Treat our entire defensive half as the danger zone.
  • Marking: Any opposing player crossing half should be marked 100%, with us close enough to touch them.
  • Communication: Remember to call out who you're marking.

We Need to Win the 50/50's

What is a 50/50 ball, coach?

A "50/50 ball" refers to a loose ball on the field that is equally accessible to players from both teams, giving each an approximately equal chance of gaining control.

If individually we conquer anticipation and determination for a 50/50, this will elevate our team’s performance.

Replays by the Goalie (Goalie Possession from Out of Bounds Over End Line or Save)

  • On Offense: It's a scoring opportunity.
  • On Defense: It's eliminating a scoring opportunity.

Creating a mentality of "my ball and no one else's ball" goes a long way.

Offensively, You May Have Heard Me Say “Go Through the Ball”

What does this mean? It means to go at the ball and not slow down.

In a 50/50 situation, what does this do?

  • Creates a fearless attitude
  • We might win a foul
  • We show our grit
  • We back them down
  • We force a bad touch by them
