Offensive Tactics

Drill Name: “Decide and Divide

    Objective:

    Teach players to make quick decisions on who takes the ball and who creates space/support, preventing bunching and improving team shape.

    Setup:

    • Create a 20x20 yard grid.
    • Divide players into groups of 3 (2 attackers + 1 defender).
    • One ball per group.

    How It Works:

    1. The two attackers start side-by-side on one end of the grid with a defender 5–10 yards in front.
    2. Coach plays a ball into space between the two attackers.
    3. One attacker must call and take ownership of the ball.
    4. The other attacker immediately separates and finds a new angle to support (not running at the ball).
    5. They attempt to complete 3 passes under light pressure from the defender.
    6. Rotate roles every 60 seconds.

    Coaching Points:

    • Communication: One player must call for the ball clearly.
    • Body Language: Be decisive—use speed and direction to show intent.
    • Support Positioning: The non-ball player should find a diagonal passing lane, not crowd the ball.
    • Defensive Pressure: Keep it light to moderate, increase as players improve.

    Progression:

    • Add a goal or end zone—if they complete 3 passes, they attack and score.
    • Play 3v2 to increase pressure and emphasize off-ball movement.
