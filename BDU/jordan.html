Fast Feet

A focused agility drill designed to enhance foot speed and ball control through rapid touch sequences. This exercise develops a player's ability to make quick, precise contact with the ball while maintaining balance and coordination. The drill emphasizes rhythmic movement patterns that improve reaction time and technical execution under pace. Essential for developing the quick feet needed in tight game situations, this foundational exercise builds crucial motor skills for advanced ball manipulation and control at speed.

First Touch Variations

Training exercise focused on developing different first touch techniques to control and manipulate the ball effectively.

Figure 8

Perform Figure 8 movements around cones using various foot techniques: • Left foot only • Right foot only • Inside of both feet • Outside of both feet • Scissors movement • V-turns • Inside of sole Each variation improves close ball control and dribbling skills.

    • Play to 5
    • Hit a cone, loose a point
    • Take more then two touches, loose a point
    • Must take it outside, pass through middle

At Home Ball Mastery Workout

Using one cone, practice foot movements controlling the ball forward, sideways, and backwards to improve basic ball mastery skills.

Three Types of First Touch

Master three essential first touches on aerial balls: cushion with the sole of your foot for close control, use the instep for directional control, and strike with your laces to redirect the ball with power.
