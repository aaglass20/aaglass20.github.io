Caicedo Turn Technique

The Caicedo Turn is a dynamic soccer maneuver that combines agility, ball control, and quick directional change. Named after the Colombian forward, this technique involves a sharp turn using the sole of the foot to drag the ball back while simultaneously pivoting the body. This move allows players to evade defenders effectively and create space for passing or shooting. Mastery of the Caicedo Turn enhances a player's ability to maintain possession under pressure and execute rapid transitions during gameplay.

Core Turning Skills Collection

A comprehensive guide to mastering five essential turning techniques: the quick Outside Turn, the controlled Inside Turn, the sharp Chop Turn, the evasive Drag Back Turn, and the dynamic Roulette Turn. Each movement serves a unique purpose in match situations, from escaping pressure to creating attacking opportunities. These fundamental skills form the foundation for effective ball control and directional changes, enabling players to navigate tight spaces and maintain possession under defensive pressure.

Reception & Control Fundamentals

Master three fundamental techniques that combine reception and movement: the efficient No Turn Touch for maintaining forward momentum, the dynamic Open Up turn for creating space and angles, and the decisive Kill & Push for instant control and acceleration. These essential skills help players make quick decisions on the ball, enabling smooth transitions from receiving to attacking while maintaining control under pressure.

Circle Drill: Reception & Turn Series

In this circle-based drill, players position themselves around strategically placed cones forming a circle. Each player receives the ball, executes a turn, and passes to the next teammate in sequence. This continuous rotation develops quick turning skills, spatial awareness, and passing accuracy while maintaining a steady rhythm. Perfect for practicing various turning techniques under the pressure of constant movement.

Turn & Accelerate: Dynamic Dribbling Series

Master the art of combining sharp turns with explosive dribbling sequences. This technique teaches players to maintain close ball control through the turn, then immediately transition into purposeful dribbling at pace. Perfect for attacking players looking to create space, beat defenders, and change direction while maintaining momentum and ball control.

Magic Turn

The Magic Turn is an advanced ball control technique that combines deception with fluid movement. Using quick footwork, players perform a subtle drag-back followed by an explosive spin move, making the ball appear to vanish from one side and reappear on the other. This elegant turn not only bewilders defenders but creates immediate space for the attacking player to exploit. Perfect for tight situations where standard turns are too predictable.

Wall Move Turns

The Wall Move Turn is a specialized technique designed for tight spaces near the sideline or against pressing defenders. By using the imaginary wall as a training aid, players learn to protect the ball while executing quick, precise turns under pressure. This drill develops close control, spatial awareness, and the ability to maintain possession when boxed in against the touchline. Essential for wingers and fullbacks who frequently operate in these challenging positions.

High Level Turns

Master three advanced turning techniques essential for creating space and beating defenders. The Cruyff Turn, a deceptive move where the player feints to shoot before dragging the ball behind their standing leg, creates immediate separation from defenders. The Inside Spin involves rapidly turning inward while protecting the ball, perfect for tight spaces and quick direction changes. The Outside Spin utilizes explosive momentum to turn away from pressure using the outside of the foot, ideal for maintaining speed while changing direction. These sophisticated turns are crucial tools for midfielders and forwards to maintain possession and create attacking opportunities under pressure.
