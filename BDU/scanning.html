Scanning

Scanning in Soccer: A Key to Better Decision-Making

1. What is Scanning?

Scanning involves a player looking around (usually over their shoulders) to gather information before they receive the ball. It helps them make quicker and more effective decisions, reducing the time they need to control, pass, dribble, or shoot.

Asking what we are scanning for, should provide this answer

  • Ball
  • Opposition
  • Teammates
  • Space

2. Why is Scanning Important?

  • Improves Awareness: Players know where their teammates and opponents are before they receive the ball.
  • Enhances Decision-Making: Helps in making quicker and more accurate decisions.
  • Reduces Risk of Losing Possession: Avoid getting caught in tight situations.
  • Creates Space and Opportunities: Position better and exploit gaps in the defense.
  • Increases Confidence and Control: Feel more comfortable receiving the ball under pressure.

3. How to Improve Scanning?

A. Increase Frequency

Top players scan 6-8 times per 10 seconds before receiving the ball.

B. Scan Before and After Receiving the Ball

  1. Before receiving – Look at teammates, opponents, and available space.
  2. While receiving – Check surroundings again to confirm previous information.
  3. After receiving – Quickly decide the next action.

C. Proper Body Positioning

Stay side-on (half-turned) to see both the ball and surroundings.

D. Practice Under Pressure

  • Play in small-sided games to develop quick decision-making.
  • Use one-touch passing drills to improve speed of play.

4. How have we worked on scanning in practice?

Keep you Head Up

Objective

To encourage players to dribble with their heads up while being aware of their surroundings.

Instructions

  1. The dribbler moves around the space, keeping control of the ball.
  2. The defender moves with them, staying close and holding up different finger combinations.
  3. The dribbler must call out the number of fingers the defender is holding up while continuing to dribble.
  4. Switch roles after 1-2 minutes.

Know your move before you move

  1. 2 players. One passer, One receiving
  2. Passing player pressures player left, right, or up the middle
  3. Receiving player does a quick scan to see where pressure is
  4. Another quick scan can happen right before touching the ball
  5. We are trying to make a decision where to go with the ball before having the ball
  6. Never stop or keep the ball in neutral
  7. Move with a direction and be confident
  8. Adjust when needed
  9. Read the pressure
