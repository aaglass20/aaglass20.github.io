Day 3: Combination Play

Quick passing to unlock defenses

Drill 1: Wall Passes (Give and Go)

10 minutes Pairs Beginner

Basic give-and-go. Pass to partner, sprint past defender, receive return pass. The most effective way to beat a defender.

  • Crisp pass to partner's front foot
  • Explode past defender immediately
  • Partner plays one-touch return
  • Call for ball when you're open

Drill 2: Third Man Runs

15 minutes Trios Intermediate

A passes to B, but the real target is C who runs off the first pass. Use the pass to trigger third player's movement.

  • Third player starts moving as ball is passed
  • Receiver knows to play quickly to third man
  • Communication essential - know who's the outlet
  • One or two touch maximum

Drill 3: Quick Combinations

15 minutes Small Groups Intermediate

Rondo-style drill but with emphasis on combinations to break out. Players must combine with 2+ passes to escape the pressing players.

  • Quick feet, quick mind
  • Always know your next pass
  • Support the ball - give passing options
  • Combine to break pressure

Drill 4: Combination Finishing

20 minutes Groups of 4 Advanced

Start from midfield, must make 3+ combination passes before shooting. Builds game-realistic attacking patterns.

  • Build up from back with combinations
  • Final ball must be quality
  • Finish with composure
  • Reset and repeat - build rhythm

Key Coaching Points

  • One Touch: Quick play prevents defenders settling
  • Movement: Pass and move, never stand still
  • Angles: Create passing angles for teammates
  • Weight of Pass: Right pace makes return pass easier
  • Communication: Talk! Let teammates know your intentions
