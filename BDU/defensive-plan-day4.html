Day 4: Pressing & Recovery

Winning the ball back and defensive transitions

Drill 1: Press Triggers

15 minutes Team Intermediate

Identify when to press: bad touch, back pass, slow pass, player facing own goal. Team practices recognizing triggers and pressing as a unit.

  • Press on bad first touch - ball away from foot
  • Press on back passes - receiver under pressure
  • Press when opponent is facing their own goal
  • First player presses, others cut passing lanes

Drill 2: Recovery Runs

15 minutes Individual/Pairs High Intensity

Practice sprinting back to recover defensive position. Ball played behind defender, they must sprint to get goal-side before attacker shoots.

  • Sprint at maximum speed - no jogging
  • Take direct line to goal, not to ball
  • Get goal-side first, then press the ball
  • Never give up on a recovery run

Drill 3: Counter-Press (Gegenpressing)

15 minutes 5v5 Advanced

5v5 possession game. When team loses ball, they have 6 seconds to win it back. If they do, they score a point. Builds habit of immediate reaction to losing possession.

  • React immediately when ball is lost
  • Nearest players swarm the ball
  • Cut off easy passes
  • Win it back in attacking areas when possible

Drill 4: Transition Game

20 minutes 7v7 Advanced

7v7 game with emphasis on transitions. When possession changes, defending team has 5 seconds to get into shape. Coach blows whistle to check shape.

  • Instant reaction to losing the ball
  • Communication during transition
  • Nearest player delays, others recover
  • Get compact and organized quickly

Key Coaching Points

  • Intensity: Pressing only works at 100% effort
  • Teamwork: Press as a unit - one person can't press alone
  • Triggers: Know when to press and when to drop
  • Recovery: Sprint back, never jog when defending
  • Mentality: Never give up on any play
