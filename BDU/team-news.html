🔵 BDU Explodes for Seven Second-Half Goals in 8–3 Win Over Euclid

Independence, OH  ·  May 3, 2026
BDU 8 – Euclid 3

Blue Devil United U12 Girls turned a tightly contested match into a dominant performance, scoring seven unanswered second-half goals to defeat Euclid 8–3 at Elmwood Park.

⚔️ First Half Battle

The opening half was a true back-and-forth struggle, with both teams fighting to gain control.

BDU broke through first with a beautiful sequence. Charlie Merker delivered a perfectly placed cross into the box where Ava Durdella-Vourliotis timed it perfectly, striking a one-time finish to give BDU a 1–0 lead.

Euclid responded quickly, capitalizing on a counterattack to even the score at 1–1 heading into halftime.

Whitney Phillips handled goalkeeping duties in the first half, keeping BDU organized during the early exchanges.

🔥 Second Half Surge

Euclid came out strong to start the second half, scoring early to take a 2–1 lead—but that moment sparked something in BDU.

The response was immediate—and relentless.

⚡ Second Half Scoring Run

  • Whitney Phillips, fresh out of goal, connected with Charlie Merker to level the score at 2–2
  • Moments later, Merker returned the favor, setting up Phillips to make it 3–2
  • Phillips struck again on a breakaway, pushing the lead to 4–2
  • Isabelle Budzowski was perfectly positioned to score her first goal of the season, assisted by Phillips (5–2)
  • Phillips added another, finishing off a hard shot that deflected off the keeper (6–2)
  • From midfield, Hannah Bechlem launched a powerful strike that the keeper couldn't handle (7–2)
  • Budzowski capped the run by following up a strong shot from Phillips and burying the rebound (8–2)

Euclid added a late goal as time expired, but the outcome was never in doubt.

⭐

Player of the Game

Whitney Phillips
  • 3 Goals, multiple assists
  • Steady in goal during the first half
  • Dominant second half from the field

💥 Game Highlights

  • Whitney Phillips – Dominant second half with multiple goals and assists
  • Charlie Merker – Key playmaker with a goal and multiple assists
  • Isabelle Budzowski – 2 goals, including her first of the season
  • Ava Durdella-Vourliotis – Opened the scoring with a clinical finish and later returned to goal to block a penalty kick, a huge momentum-saving moment
  • Hannah Bechlem – Long-range goal and strong presence across the field
  • JoJo Rollins, Mara Buntura, and Josie DiGeronimo – Outstanding defensive effort, locking down the outside and consistently shutting down Euclid's attack with smart positioning, physical play, and timely clearances
💪 Team Takeaway

After a tightly contested first half, BDU showed what they are capable of when they play with energy, confidence, and relentless pressure. The second-half performance was a complete team effort, turning defense into attack and capitalizing on every opportunity.

Match Report

🔵 BDU Breaks Through with 5–3 Win Over Geauga

Independence, OH  ·  April 19, 2026
BDU 5 – Geauga 3
⚡ Fast Start Sets the Tone

Claire Brandt opened the scoring early, continuing her relentless attack. After narrowly missing on a couple of chances—including a shot off the post—Claire stayed aggressive and finished unassisted to give BDU a 1–0 lead.

Moments later, JoJo Rollins used her signature long throw to create another opportunity, placing a perfect ball into the box. Charlie Merker was ready, finishing cleanly to put BDU up 2–0.

Geauga responded with a goal to cut the lead to 2–1, but BDU kept pressing. Whitney Phillips, who was applying constant pressure all game, broke free on a breakaway and calmly finished to extend the lead to 3–1 at halftime.

🔥 Second Half Push

BDU wasted no time after the break. Early in the second half, Phillips again made her impact felt, finding Emery Osysko with a well-placed pass. Emery finished with confidence, pushing the lead to 4–1.

Geauga Federation fought back, capitalizing on a scramble in the box to make it 4–2, but BDU answered once again. On a great transition play, Emma Barwacz launched a strong punt that found Phillips streaking downfield. Whitney controlled it and buried her second goal of the day, extending the lead to 5–2.

Geauga added one final goal late, but BDU held firm as the final whistle blew—securing a 5–3 victory.

💪 Team Effort Leads the Way

This match was defined by teamwork and persistence in the attacking third. BDU consistently followed up opportunities, applied pressure, and created chances through effort and smart play. Defensively, the team held strong, anchored by Hannah Bechlem and Emma Barwacz, who helped organize the back line and limit high-quality chances.
