Corner Kick

Near Defender Run

A dynamic corner kick variation where the defender makes a well-timed run from the back to attack the near post area. The defender times their run to meet the incoming corner kick with a one-touch finish, catching the opposing defense off-guard. This tactical play is particularly effective as defenders are typically not marked closely, allowing them to exploit the element of surprise.

A fundamental approach to corner kick execution that establishes core positioning and roles for maximum effectiveness. This setup demonstrates essential elements of a successful corner kick, including proper ball delivery, strategic player positioning, and basic movement patterns. Understanding these fundamentals is crucial as corner kicks represent valuable scoring opportunities in match situations. 3-1 Corner Set

Three attackers line up at far goal box corner: one makes near-post run, one cuts middle, third cleans up loose balls. Center mid hovers at box top for rebounds while defenders fill spaces above for support and coverage.

    This corner kick setup utilizes three attackers positioned at the far corner of the goal box in a coordinated running pattern. The first player makes a near-post run, the second attacks the middle of the box, while the third player positions as a "garbage man" for any deflections or loose balls. The center midfielder maintains a strategic position at the top of the box for secondary balls and defensive coverage. Defensive players systematically fill remaining spaces above the play, ensuring both attacking support and counter-attack prevention.
