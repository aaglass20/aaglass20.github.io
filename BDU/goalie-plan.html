Goalie Plan

Key Goalkeeper Skills

  1. Reflexes – Quick reaction speed to stop close-range shots and deflections.
  2. Positioning – Being in the right place based on angles, defenders, and the ball.
  3. Handling – Securely catching or controlling the ball to reduce rebounds.
  4. Communication – Giving clear, confident instructions to organize the defense.
  5. Footwork – Fast, efficient movement to stay balanced and ready for saves.
  6. Distribution – Accurate throws, passes, and punts to build attacks or relieve pressure.
  7. Aerial Ability – Confidently dealing with crosses, corners, and high balls.
  8. Courage – Willingness to challenge attackers and dive at feet when needed.
  9. Decision-Making – Making quick, correct choices about when to come out, stay, catch, or punch.
  10. Mental Toughness – Staying focused, shaking off mistakes, and performing under pressure.

Communication

Giving clear, confident instructions to organize the defense.

Goalkeepers must be vocal leaders. Use short, clear, confident commands:

  • "Keeper!" – When claiming the ball
  • "Away!" – Clear the ball
  • "Step!" – Push the defensive line up
  • "Drop!" – Fall back into position
  • "Man on!" – Teammate is under pressure
  • "Mark up!" – Assign defenders to attackers

Be loud, confident, and decisive—your voice prevents confusion and mistakes.

Distribution

Accurate throws, passes, and punts to build attacks or relieve pressure.

Throwing

Use an overarm throw for quick counterattacks, or a sidearm roll for precise distribution to nearby teammates.

Punting

For long clearances, drop the ball at waist height and strike with the laces. Consider wind conditions and the position of teammates.

Punting

Goal Kicks

Use a firm follow-through and a consistent technique to deliver accurate goal kicks. Consider playing short if under pressure.

Drop to Feet

When rolling the ball out, ensure a soft, accurate delivery. Use this when starting a controlled buildup.

If Team Plays It Back (Feet Only)

Stay calm when receiving back-passes. Work on your first touch and passing accuracy, and avoid unnecessary risks under pressure.
