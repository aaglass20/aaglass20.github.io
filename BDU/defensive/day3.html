Day 3: Team Shape

Defending as a unit - communication and positioning

Drill 1: Defensive Line Movement

15 minutes Back 4 Intermediate

Practice moving as a defensive line - stepping up together, dropping together, and sliding across the field as a unit.

  • Move as one unit - no gaps between players
  • Constant communication - "step!", "drop!", "slide!"
  • Watch the ball and your teammates
  • Maintain arm's length distance between defenders

Drill 2: Compact Shape

15 minutes Team Intermediate

Full team practices staying compact. Distance between lines should be 25-30 yards maximum. Ball moves, team shifts as a block.

  • Stay connected - defense to midfield to attack
  • Shift as ball moves side to side
  • Compress toward the ball
  • Protect the central areas

Drill 3: Cover and Balance

15 minutes Back 4 Intermediate

When one defender presses, others must cover. Practice the angles and distances for providing cover.

  • Cover defender positions at 45-degree angle
  • Not too close, not too far (4-6 yards)
  • Ready to step in if pressing defender is beaten
  • Balance defender protects far post

Drill 4: 7v7 Shape Game

20 minutes 7v7 Advanced

7v7 game where defending team scores points for maintaining shape. Coach calls "freeze" at random moments - good shape = 1 point.

  • Constant awareness of team shape
  • Communicate positioning to teammates
  • Quick recovery after losing ball
  • Transition to defensive shape immediately

Key Coaching Points

  • Communication: Constant talking - "step", "drop", "left", "right"
  • Compactness: Stay tight as a unit, reduce space for opponents
  • Cover: Always have someone ready to help the pressing defender
  • Transition: Get into shape immediately when losing possession
