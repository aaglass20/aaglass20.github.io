Day 3: Distribution

Starting attacks with accurate distribution

Drill 1: Rolling the Ball

10 minutes With Targets Beginner

Practice rolling ball to teammates' feet. Quick, accurate, and on the ground. The safest distribution method for short distances.

  • Bowl the ball like bowling - smooth release
  • Keep it on the ground
  • Aim for receiver's front foot
  • Quick release - don't hold ball too long

Drill 2: Throwing Technique

15 minutes With Targets Intermediate

Overarm throws for medium distance. Ball should arrive at good height for teammate to control. Accuracy over power.

  • Step into the throw
  • Release at highest point
  • Follow through toward target
  • Aim for chest height arrival

Drill 3: Goal Kicks

15 minutes Individual Intermediate

Practice goal kicks - both long and short options. Technique focus: plant foot, strike through ball, follow through.

  • Plant foot beside ball, pointed at target
  • Strike through center-bottom of ball for height
  • Lock ankle, use laces
  • Full follow through
  • Practice short goal kicks to defenders too

Drill 4: Distribution Under Pressure

20 minutes Game Situation Advanced

Make a save, then immediately distribute to an open player while opponent pressures. Decision making: who is open? Roll, throw, or kick?

  • Scan before you catch - know your options
  • Quick decision - don't hold ball
  • Short pass if under pressure (safe option)
  • Counter-attack if space is available (quick throw)
  • Communicate - call for the ball or for space

Key Coaching Points

  • Accuracy First: Better to keep possession than bomb it long
  • Quick Release: Don't hold ball - start attacks fast
  • Read the Game: Know where to play before you catch
  • Short Option: Playing short to feet is often the best choice
  • Counter-Attack: Quick throw can catch opponents out of position
