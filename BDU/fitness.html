Relay Drills

Competitive team-based exercises that combine multiple soccer skills in a relay race format.

Circle Relay

A competitive team drill combining sprinting and ball control, where players must sprint between points and demonstrate controlled dribbling in a relay format.

    • Two teams
    • Start at middle
    • Run one way, touch end
    • Sprint to the other end
    • Dribble ball to other circle and stop
    • Next group goes once ball rest
    Why is this important?
    • Fitness
    • Control
    • Relay
    • Head up dribbling

1 V 1 Dribble Relay

A competitive dribbling drill where players navigate through cones in a race format, with optional variations including ring transfers to add complexity and decision-making elements.

    • Two lines
    • Dribble and around each pair of cones
    • Go around pole, race back
    • Once line crossed, next goes
    • Start as drill before racint
    Variations
    • Put Agility Pole at finsh
    • Put Rings on Poles
    • Player must take one ring from one pole and transfer to the other

Tap Relay

Quick-paced relay drill where players perform toe taps on multiple balls before sprinting back, combining ball control practice with speed and agility.

    • Toe Tap Each ball (1,5, or 10 times)
    • Sprint back after last

3 Gate Dribble Relay

A high-energy team relay where players dribble through three gates before sprinting back. Players must demonstrate precise ball control while navigating through each gate, then accelerate back to tag their teammate. This competitive drill combines technical dribbling skills with speed and quick transitions, making it perfect for developing both control and athletic conditioning.

3 Pre season fittness drills

Three dynamic drills to enhance fitness and ball control: Wind sprints build explosive speed and recovery, diagonal running develops multidirectional movement with and without the ball, and minefield challenges agility and precision as players navigate through scattered obstacles. These drills combine to improve stamina, quick direction changes, and technical skills under physical stress.

6 Exercises To Run Efficiently

A series of dynamic leg exercises including short bouncing movements, controlled skips, leg extensions, high-knee ankle lifts, alternating switches, and scissor-like leg movements. These drills improve coordination, agility, and leg strength.
