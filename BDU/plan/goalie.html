Goalkeeper Guide

Fundamentals

Hand Position

Keep your hands in a "ready" position—palms facing forward, fingers slightly spread, and thumbs forming a "W" shape. This ensures you have control when catching the ball.

Ready Position

Feet and Shoulders

Angles

Stay off your goal line and cut down angles by stepping forward. The closer you are to the shooter, the less goal they see, forcing a difficult shot.

Angles

Be on Your Toes

Keep a slight bounce in your stance, weight on the balls of your feet, ready to react quickly to shots, crosses, and through balls.

Positioning

Align yourself between the ball and the center of the goal. Always adjust your position with each pass to maintain optimal coverage.

Communication

Goalkeepers must be vocal leaders. Use short, clear, confident commands:

  • “Keeper!” – When claiming the ball
  • “Away!” – Clear the ball
  • “Step!” – Push the defensive line up
  • “Drop!” – Fall back into position
  • “Man on!” – Teammate is under pressure
  • “Mark up!” – Assign defenders to attackers

Be loud, confident, and decisive—your voice prevents confusion and mistakes.

Diving

Diving Drills

Develop quick reflexes and lateral movement with diving drills. Focus on:

  • Powerful push-offs with your legs
  • Leading with your hands to secure the ball
  • Landing safely to avoid injury

Defending Corners

Be aggressive but smart. Position yourself slightly off the goal line and scan for threats. Decide early whether to catch, punch, or stay back.

Defending the Cross

Time your movements and judge the flight of the ball. High balls should be caught at their highest point, while dangerous crosses may require punching away to clear danger.

Distribution

Throwing

Use an overarm throw for quick counterattacks, or a sidearm roll for precise distribution to nearby teammates.

Punting

For long clearances, drop the ball at waist height and strike with the laces. Consider wind conditions and the position of teammates.

Goal Kicks

Use a firm follow-through and a consistent technique to deliver accurate goal kicks. Consider playing short if under pressure.

Drop to Feet

When rolling the ball out, ensure a soft, accurate delivery. Use this when starting a controlled buildup.

If Team Plays It Back (Feet Only)

Stay calm when receiving back-passes. Work on your first touch and passing accuracy, and avoid unnecessary risks under pressure.

Tactics & Leadership

Communication

Constantly communicate with your defense. Call for the ball early and clearly, and organize the backline to prevent dangerous situations.

Field General

As a goalkeeper, you see the entire field—use this vision to direct teammates, warn of threats, and control defensive shape.

Playing to the Outside

Encourage defenders to force attackers wide, reducing the risk of central shots and increasing the chance of defensive recovery.

Goalie Ready Position (Expanded)

Hand Position

Keep your hands in a “ready” position:

  • Palms facing slightly forward or at a slight outward angle
  • Fingers naturally curved and slightly spread
  • Thumbs pointing toward each other, forming a relaxed “W” shape
  • Hands positioned just outside shoulder width, at chest height
This posture prepares the goalie to react quickly to high, low, or lateral shots.

Foot Positioning & Body Posture

  • Feet shoulder-width apart or slightly wider
  • Knees slightly bent
  • Weight on the balls of your feet (not heels)
  • Chest forward, hips slightly back, back straight but relaxed

When the Ball Is on the Other Side of the Field (Far Offensive Third)

  • Feet can be closer together, relaxed but alert
  • Stand slightly outside of the 6-yard box (sweeper-keeper position)
  • Body upright, scanning the field, hands relaxed
  • Communicate with defenders: remind them to stay marked and organized
  • Ready to intercept through balls or distribute quickly

When the Ball Is at Midfield

  • Feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent
  • Stand at the top of the 18-yard box or just inside it
  • Hands raised to chest height, body in semi-crouch
  • Monitor defensive spacing and threats
  • Use vocal commands: “Mark up!”, “Watch the runner!”, “Drop!”, “Step!”

When the Ball Is Near the Box or in the Defensive Third

  • Drop into a fully ready stance
  • Feet slightly wider than shoulders, knees bent
  • Hands up and forward, eyes on the ball
  • Stay light on your feet with small bounces
  • Adjust body angle based on attack direction (near post, top of box, wide angles)

Angles & Positioning

  • Think: goal – ball – you (always in line)
  • Step forward to cut angles as attackers approach
  • Use lateral shuffles to stay square to the ball (don’t cross your feet)
  • Move diagonally with ball as it shifts across the field
