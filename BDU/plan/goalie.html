Goalkeeper Guide

Fundamentals

Hand Position

Keep your hands in a "ready" position—palms facing forward, fingers slightly spread, and thumbs forming a "W" shape. This ensures you have control when catching the ball.

Angles

Stay off your goal line and cut down angles by stepping forward. The closer you are to the shooter, the less goal they see, forcing a difficult shot.

Be on Your Toes

Keep a slight bounce in your stance, weight on the balls of your feet, ready to react quickly to shots, crosses, and through balls.

Positioning

Align yourself between the ball and the center of the goal. Always adjust your position with each pass to maintain optimal coverage.

Defensive Skills

Diving Drills

Develop quick reflexes and lateral movement with diving drills. Focus on:

  • Powerful push-offs with your legs
  • Leading with your hands to secure the ball
  • Landing safely to avoid injury

Diving and reaction drill

Defending Corners

Be aggressive but smart. Position yourself slightly off the goal line and scan for threats. Decide early whether to catch, punch, or stay back.

Defending the Cross

Time your movements and judge the flight of the ball. High balls should be caught at their highest point, while dangerous crosses may require punching away to clear danger.

Distribution

Throwing

Use an overarm throw for quick counterattacks, or a sidearm roll for precise distribution to nearby teammates.

Punting

For long clearances, drop the ball at waist height and strike with the laces. Consider wind conditions and the position of teammates.

Goal Kicks

Use a firm follow-through and a consistent technique to deliver accurate goal kicks. Consider playing short if under pressure.

Drop to Feet

When rolling the ball out, ensure a soft, accurate delivery. Use this when starting a controlled buildup.

If Team Plays It Back (Feet Only)

Stay calm when receiving back-passes. Work on your first touch and passing accuracy, and avoid unnecessary risks under pressure.

Tactics & Leadership

Communication

Constantly communicate with your defense. Call for the ball early and clearly, and organize the backline to prevent dangerous situations.

Field General

As a goalkeeper, you see the entire field—use this vision to direct teammates, warn of threats, and control defensive shape.

Playing to the Outside

Encourage defenders to force attackers wide, reducing the risk of central shots and increasing the chance of defensive recovery.
