Defense

Defense Training

Focus on defending skills, drills, and strategies.

Warm-Ups

Run Like a Defender

  • Run like a Defender (warmup)
  • Now really run like a defender (short, intense run)
  • Channel like a Defender

Defensive Drills

Touch and Feel Drill

1 player bodies up and keeps the other player away from the ball.
2nd player does what they can to take the ball away.

Both players work on defensive skills:

  • Player 1: Using physicality to defend the ball.
  • Player 2: Using physicality to take the ball.

Start with 10 seconds, work up to 30.

Scanning Drills

Scanning is observing your environment to explore all options. It helps you:

  • Call out what is not covered.
  • Predict and communicate your next moves to teammates.
  • Constantly scan before deciding:
    • Can I dribble?
    • Should I take a wide touch?
    • Should I take my time?
    • Should I move?
    • Should I hit a lane to call on a runner?
    • Should I hit their feet?

Communication

Defensive communication keeps the team organized:

  • Calling out your mark
  • Calling out open marks
  • Calling for the ball
  • Calling out where the ball is going
  • Other defensive communications?

Order of Field Generals

  • Goalie
  • Center D
  • Center M
  • All Team Members

Defensive Tactics

Play to the outside is our primary goal, but faking to go inside is an option.
Switching fields is also an option (even in the back) - we want to be that team.

  • Hitting lanes, hitting feet
  • Remember the other team knows we want to go outside

Levels of Guarding

  • Inside goal box: Touching player
  • ¼ of field: 1-2 arm's length
  • ¼ to half of the field: 2 arm's length

Horizontal Passing & Touch Drills

  • Wide touch
  • Short touch
  • Clearing in the box

Fire Drill

Urgency: Get the ball like your life depends on it.

  • Channel, delay, defend
  • Wait for a mistake (like a game of chess)
  • Know where you are on the field and when you can’t wait anymore

Decision-Making Drills

Drills that make you choose when to:

  • Dribble
  • Pass
  • Hit through balls
  • Hit feet
  • Switch fields

Switch off defenders, recognize when to:

  • Help if your teammate is beat
  • No when to help (recognize 2v1 situations, etc.)

50/50 Balls

Action trapping, learning possession, making runs from the back.
