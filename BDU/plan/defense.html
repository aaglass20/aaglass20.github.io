Defensive Drills

Touch and Feel Drill

1 player bodies up and keeps the other player away from the ball.
2nd player does what they can to take the ball away.

Both players work on defensive skills:

  • Player 1: Using physicality to defend the ball.
  • Player 2: Using physicality to take the ball.

Start with 10 seconds, work up to 30.

Scanning Drills

Scanning is observing your environment to explore all options. It helps you:

  • Call out what is not covered.
  • Predict and communicate your next moves to teammates.
  • Constantly scan before deciding:
    • Can I dribble?
    • Should I take a wide touch?
    • Should I take my time?
    • Should I move?
    • Should I hit a lane to call on a runner?
    • Should I hit their feet?

Summary on what we are scanning for:

  • Ball
  • Opposition
  • Teammates
  • Space

Know your move before you move - scan

Defensive Grit

Body Positioning and Use of Contact

Shoulder-to-Shoulder

This is permitted when both players are within playing distance of the ball and neither player is using excessive force. Work on timing your shoulder contact to unbalance the attacker just as they receive or control the ball.

Leverage Your Body

  • Use your torso and arms (within reason) to guide attackers away from the ball.
  • Keep elbows in and forearms tight to avoid fouls for pushing or holding. Instead, engage with your shoulders and chest to angle the opponent off the ball.

Jockeying and Shepherding

  • Maintain a low, stable stance and keep your center of gravity over your feet.
  • Move laterally and apply controlled pressure, pushing attackers away from dangerous areas without overcommitting.

Winning Aerial Duels, 50/50

  • Legal Arm Use: Use arms for balance but do not extend or swing them in a way that endangers the opponent. It is about occupying space and winning position.
  • Use body to leverage space
  • On their goal kicks, its good to stay behind and anticipate. Body up sometimes.

Tackle Technique

Slide Tackle Basics

  • Aim to make contact with the ball first; your tackling leg should be extended and your foot pointing toward the ball.
  • Keep one foot on the ground if possible, and avoid coming in from behind or with studs up, as these can be deemed reckless.

Standing or Block Tackle

  • Approach the attacker at an angle. Plant your standing leg strongly and drive the tackling leg through the ball.
  • Keep your upper body firm to absorb contact and disrupt the opponent’s balance.
  • Stay on you Toes
  • Look for the mistake

Timing and Patience

The most physical defenders often excel at anticipation and timing, rather than constantly going to ground. Wait for the right moment when an attacker’s touch is slightly off or they have limited control of the ball.

Mental and Tactical Approach

Assertiveness and Confidence

  • Step into challenges decisively; hesitation often leads to missed tackles or late, mistimed fouls.
  • Communicate with teammates; a coordinated defensive line that compresses space can create more opportunities for strong, legal challenges.

Pressure and Delay

  • Closing down quickly (but under control) forces attackers to make decisions faster.
  • By pressuring them, you often gain the upper hand physically since they cannot settle and prepare for contact.

Training Drills to Increase Physicality

1v1 Shoulder-to-Shoulder Drill

Set up a small grid or channel. One player dribbles down the channel while the defender must use body positioning to push them off the line or make a legal tackle. Focus on keeping the arms tight and using the shoulder and upper body for contact.

Shielding and Dispossession

Pair players up. One player shields the ball; the other tries to dispossess. The defender focuses on staying low, using the body to push the shielding player off balance, and cleanly poking the ball away.

How to become more aggressive

  • Work on being physical first
  • The work on protecting and escaping
  • Work on hand and body techniques

Start with 10 seconds, work up to 30.

Aerial Challenge Drill

Coach or teammate serves lofted balls into a designated area. Two defenders compete to win the header or control it on the chest. Emphasize timing, vertical leap, and legal use of arms for balance.

Small-Sided Games with Tight Space

Play 3v3 or 4v4 in a smaller area to encourage close contact and physical battles. This format forces defenders to be quicker and more decisive in applying body pressure.
