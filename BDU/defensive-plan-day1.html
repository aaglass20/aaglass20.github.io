Day 1: Defensive Stance & Positioning

Foundation drills for proper defensive technique

Drill 1: Defensive Stance Practice

10 minutes Individual Beginner

Players practice holding the proper defensive stance. Low center of gravity, on balls of feet, arms out for balance, eyes on the ball.

  • Knees bent, hips low
  • Weight on balls of feet, ready to move
  • Arms out wide for balance
  • Side-on to attacker, not square
  • Eyes on the ball, not the player's feet

Drill 2: Shadow Defending

15 minutes Pairs Beginner

One player moves laterally while the partner mirrors their movement, maintaining proper defensive position. No ball - focus on footwork.

  • Quick shuffle steps, don't cross feet
  • Stay balanced and low
  • Keep proper distance from partner
  • React quickly to direction changes

Drill 3: Closing Down

15 minutes Pairs Intermediate

Attacker receives a pass, defender closes down quickly then slows approach to get in proper position. Focus on speed of approach and controlled finish.

  • Sprint while ball is traveling
  • Slow down as you get close (last 2-3 yards)
  • Get into side-on stance
  • Jockey the attacker toward the sideline
  • Be patient - don't dive in

Banana Run

Drill 4: Positioning Game

20 minutes Small Groups Intermediate

3v3 game in a small area. Defenders must stay goal-side of their mark at all times. Points awarded for maintaining position, points lost for getting beaten.

  • Always know where your mark is
  • Stay goal-side and ball-side
  • Communicate with teammates
  • Recover quickly if beaten

Key Coaching Points

  • Patience: Don't dive in - make the attacker make a mistake
  • Body Shape: Side-on position limits attacker's options
  • Communication: Defenders must talk to each other
  • Recovery: If beaten, sprint to recover goal-side
  • Eyes on Ball: Watch the ball, not the player's body feints
