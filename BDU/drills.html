Drills

Simple Grid Drill

A dynamic grid-based exercise where three attackers navigate through three zones, facing defenders in the first and last grids. Focuses on tactical movement, communication, and turning skills while maintaining possession to reach the scoring zone.

3 Person line pass and move

Linear passing exercise with three players focusing on constant movement and varied pass distances. Emphasizes one-touch passing technique while alternating between medium-long and short passes, with players continuously repositioning after each pass.

  • Medium long
  • Move
  • Pass Back
  • Short Pass Back
  • Move
  • 1 Touch is focus, but use 2 if needed
Why is this important?
  • Moving every pass, short and medium

Pass, Shoot, Defend

Quick-paced rotation drill combining passing, shooting, and defensive elements. Players cycle through roles: initiating with short passes and drops, executing shots on goal, and defending shots, emphasizing communication and rapid transitions between positions.

  • Short pass forward, Communicate to recipient
  • Call for Drop
  • Rush to dropped ball
  • Quick pass over
  • Top player shoots
  • Defender trys to block
  • After shot, shooter becomes defender
  • Initial passer becomes shooter
  • Defender jumps back in line
Why is this important?
  • Urgency as passer, shooter, defender
  • works on short dump or drop
  • Communications and movement

Work Sides, Play Through

Full-team progression drill focused on building play from the defensive third through the sides. Emphasizes defensive turning decisions, pressure response, communication between players, and advancing the ball effectively under varying levels of pressure. Can be adapted with additional defensive elements and goalkeeper.

  • D1 pass to outside
  • D2 Turn to outside
  • D2 takes ball forward
  • Coach then pressures D2 to force pass
  • D2 passes up line or plays through
  • Nears M or F communicates they have it
  • The other separates
  • Work ball down field for shot
  • Could add Goalie
  • Could add Defender
Why is this important?
  • Works on importance of playing outside
  • Works on turning out, instead of turning in
  • Works on carrying ball when not pressure
  • Works on keeping head up to look for pressure
  • Works on releasing ball with pressure
  • Can have teammates identify pressure to help teammates
  • Works through ball and communicating the intended receiver
  • Works simple pass and finish down the filed

Hold the line

A drill that focuses on the importance of holding the line. Includes a simple pass and finish down the field.

  • Line 3 players up at midfield
  • Line up 3 Defenders
  • Coach playes ball between the lanes to create situations
  • Another Coach behind goalies
  • Scneario 1: play ball through defense, goalie reaction, boot ball to side or out of bounds
  • Scenario 2: play ball to either side, if midfield gets beyond defence, switch off help
  • Coach can help players to indentify backside help
  • backside help should go directly to ball and stat defending closely, attempting to retrieve
Why is this important?
  • Goalie reacting to bail out defense, goalie should call off D
  • Work on help and communication on defense
  • Defense should get legally handsy

Ladder, Side to Side, Dribble

Three-station rotation drill focusing on footwork, ball control, and dribbling skills. Includes side-to-side passing, ladder drills, and dribbling exercises with speed variations.

  • Side to Side (1 or 2 touch) back and forth to coach - Work both feet
  • Ladder (1 or 2 touch) back and forth to coach - Work both feet
  • Dribble with both feet, speed, 4 rollovers with gate, Long touch with Sprint Form
Why is this important?
  • General skills

Dynamic Pass-to-Play Decision Drill

Dynamic transition drill where success or failure of an initial pass through gates determines player roles. Successful passes lead to offensive opportunities, while missed passes force immediate defensive transition. Emphasizes quick decision-making, precise passing, and rapid role switching, with goalkeeper involvement adding game-realistic finishing scenarios.

  • Goal throws ball to first player
  • Player attempts to pass through gate
  • If pass goes through gate, take off an become an option for leading pass
  • Try to pass before next gate to teamate that should be moving after the pass
  • If Player misses first gate, Second Player becomes offence
  • First player immediatly had to transition from Offense to Defense
  • P2 is tring to score on keeper
  • P1 is trying to defend, not waiting on it
Why is this important?
  • P1 is transition to D to O if they make a good pass
  • P1 is transitioning quickly back to D if they make a bad pass
  • Promotes movement on O side if successful
  • There is a consequence for a bad pass

Verbal Cue Response Drill

Interactive communication drill where partners practice verbal and visual directional cues, combining technical skills with tactical awareness. Players respond to specific calls with corresponding movements and touches, incorporating directional play, pressure responses, and turning sequences, all executed with urgency.

  • Partner yells out call
  • 1: Go Right, Say Right and point Right. Player touches right, plays back (2 touch)
  • 2: Go Left, Say Left and point Left. Player touches left, plays back (2 touch)
  • Pressure: Play back(drop), say pressure (or man on), 1 touch
  • Turn: Say turn, Player uses a turn moves forward and shoots
  • Be urgent
Why is this important?
  • Works on player to player communication
  • Works on touch, turns, movement

Touch-Turn-Pass Progression

Technical drill combining footwork, receiving, and turning skills around a fixed obstacle. Players maintain active movement with shuffling before executing a sequence of ball reception, turn, wall pass, and return play, emphasizing proper first touch and continuous movement on toes.

  • Use pole as obstacle
  • Shuffle back an forth on toes behind pole
  • Recieve ball, turn, play wall
  • Go around pole, play back to teamate or coach
  • Repeat
Why is this important?
  • Keeps constant movement, on toes
  • Works turn, receive
  • Simple first touch

Reactive Directional Control

Quick-response drill combining physical readiness and directional ball control. Players maintain active footwork while facing away from the coach, then react to verbal commands by turning and executing specific ball movements. Emphasizes rapid reaction time, first touch control, and directional awareness while responding to verbal cues.

  • Play on toes, quick feet, runs in place, facing away from coach
  • Coach yells Left, Right, Drop
  • On call, player turs around as ball is played
  • Right, take to their right, 1 touch out, pass back
  • Left, take to their left, 1 touch out, pass back
  • Drop,1 touch pass back
Why is this important?
  • Work on communication
  • Reaction
  • Drops
  • touch outside with control

Protect the Ball

Two-player drill focused on using body position to protect and challenge for the ball, lasting 10-30 seconds per round.

    First player bodies up and keeps the other player away from the ball.
    2nd player does what they can to take the ball away.

    Both players work on defensive skills:

    • Player 1: Using physicality to defend the ball.
    • Player 2: Using physicality to take the ball.

    Start with 10 seconds, work up to 30.

Midfield Interception Drill

Solo training using a rebound wall to practice interception timing, quick movement, and finishing to a small goal.
