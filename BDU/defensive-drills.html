Progressive Defensive Containment Drill

Advanced defensive training progression focusing on proper body positioning and controlled tackling techniques. Starting with warm-up drills and channeling exercises, defenders learn to maintain proper hip alignment, apply strategic pressure, and execute controlled tackles. Emphasizes body-first defensive approach over poking, building from guided instruction to independent execution while developing confident defensive positioning and effective tackle timing.

  • Offence slow dribbles
  • Warm up with "Flip" drill
  • Defense channels with "running like a Defender"
  • Shift hips if Off changes direction
  • Coach yells attack, attack
  • Lunge across Offences body
  • Arm first, post on body
  • Step across body and tackle
  • We look for openings to steal
  • Try it without coach influence
Why is this important?
  • Stepping across body slows down offence if you miss your tackle attempt
  • We don't poke, we body up
  • Be confident

Interception Drill

Training exercise focusing on defensive anticipation and timing to step in front of passes for key interceptions.

Touch & Feel

A dynamic drill involving four players practicing passing under pressure, where players alternate between passing roles and applying defensive touch pressure, developing ball control and spatial awareness while maintaining passing accuracy.

    • Two players pass back and forth
    • As one player receives the ball another player run from cone and touches them.
    • Then the player passes to the other player, Player moves back to cond
    • New player with ball has a 4th player run up and touch them

Multi-Ball Deception Game

A dynamic one-on-one shooting drill using multiple cones with balls, where attackers use deceptive moves to create scoring opportunities while defenders work to maintain defensive positioning and prevent shots.
