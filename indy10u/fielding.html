Fielding Fundamentals

Build confidence in the field with proper technique for ground balls, fly balls, and throwing.

Key Fielding Fundamentals

  • 1

    Ready Position

    Athletic stance with feet wider than shoulders, knees bent, weight forward on balls of feet. Glove out front, eyes on hitter.

  • 2

    Ground Ball Approach

    Charge the ball when possible. Get low with butt down, field the ball out front between your feet. "Alligator" hands - top hand covers.

  • 3

    Fly Ball Technique

    First step is back on balls hit in the air. Get behind the ball, catch above forehead with two hands. Call "I got it!"

  • 4

    Throwing Mechanics

    Grip ball with 4-seam grip. Reach back, lead with elbow, follow through toward target. Step toward your target.

  • 5

    Footwork

    Quick feet beat quick hands. After fielding, crow hop to generate momentum toward your target for a stronger throw.

Practice Drills

Rollers

Partner rolls ground balls from 15-20 feet away. Focus on getting low, fielding out front, and making accurate throws back.

Pop Flies

Partner throws high fly balls. Practice drop step, tracking the ball, and catching above the forehead. Work on communication.

Triangle Drill

3 players form triangle. Field and throw around the triangle. Focus on quick transfer and accurate throws. Add timing.

Barehand Drill

Field slow rollers without a glove. Develops soft hands and proper fielding position. Great for building confidence.

Pro Tips for Better Fielding

  • Always know the situation before the pitch - how many outs, where are the runners, what's your play?
  • Field the ball, don't let it field you - be aggressive and charge when possible
  • Keep your glove low - it's easier to come up than go down
  • Use two hands whenever possible - the throwing hand secures the ball for a quick transfer
