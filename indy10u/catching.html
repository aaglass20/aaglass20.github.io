Catching Fundamentals

Become the leader of your defense with strong receiving, blocking, and throwing skills behind the plate.

Key Catching Fundamentals

  • 1

    Stance

    With no runners: relaxed stance, one knee can be down for comfort. With runners: athletic stance, ready to throw. Give pitcher a good target.

  • 2

    Receiving

    Soft hands - catch the ball, don't stab at it. Frame pitches by catching and holding position. Quiet body, active glove.

  • 3

    Blocking

    Drop to knees, fill the gap with body. Glove between legs, chin tucked. Keep ball in front of you - don't try to catch it.

  • 4

    Throwing

    Quick transfer from glove to throwing hand. Pop up from stance, step toward target. Quick feet, quick release. Accuracy over power.

  • 5

    Communication

    Catchers are the eyes of the field. Call plays, direct traffic, encourage your pitcher. Be loud and confident!

Practice Drills

Soft Hands Drill

Practice receiving tennis balls or softer balls without a glove. Develops soft, quiet hands that absorb the pitch.

Blocking Drill

Coach throws balls in the dirt from short distance. Practice dropping and keeping ball in front. Work on recovery.

Pop-Throw Drill

Practice the pop-up and throw motion. Focus on quick feet and transfer. Time yourself to track improvement.

Framing Drill

Receive pitches and hold position to show umpire. Work on catching borderline pitches and making them look like strikes.

Pro Tips for Better Catching

  • Be your pitcher's best friend - encourage them, keep them calm, help them succeed
  • Always know the game situation - outs, count, where runners are, what the next play should be
  • Protect your body - wear all your gear in practice just like games
  • Work on your conditioning - catching is demanding! Strong legs make everything easier
