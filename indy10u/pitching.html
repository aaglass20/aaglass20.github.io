Windmill Pitching Basics

  • 1

    Starting Position

    Both feet on the pitching rubber, shoulders square to catcher. Ball in glove, hands together at waist. Focus on target.

  • 2

    The Arm Circle

    Arm moves in a full windmill motion. Keep arm close to body, elbow straight through the circle. Brush hip at release.

  • 3

    Drive & Stride

    Push off the rubber with back foot. Stride toward home plate with front foot landing closed. Drive creates power.

  • 4

    Release Point

    Release at the hip as arm brushes past leg. Wrist snaps downward. Consistent release point = consistent accuracy.

  • 5

    Follow Through

    After release, arm continues upward. End in fielding position, ready to react. Don't stop the arm - let it flow.

Practice Drills

K Drill (Kneeling)

Kneel on throwing-side knee. Practice arm circle and release without legs. Isolates upper body mechanics.

Wrist Snaps

Partner holds glove at hip height. Practice just the wrist snap release. Develop feel for the snap that creates spin.

Walk-Through

Start behind rubber, walk into pitch. Helps timing and coordination. Focus on smooth, connected movements.

Target Practice

Set up targets in the strike zone. Work on hitting specific spots. Track accuracy percentage. Compete with yourself!

Pro Tips for Better Pitching

  • Start slow - learn the motion correctly before adding speed. Muscle memory is hard to undo!
  • Focus on one thing at a time - don't try to fix everything at once
  • Pitch to a catcher or net daily, even just 20-30 pitches keeps the motion fresh
  • Video yourself! Watching your mechanics helps identify areas to improve
