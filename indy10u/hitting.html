Master your swing mechanics, timing, and bat control to become a consistent hitter.

5 Essential Batting Drills

These five drills will help you develop proper swing mechanics, improve bat speed, and build consistency at the plate.

Tee Work Drill Breakdown

Set up tee where you want to hit (front foot with stride position).

  • 1

    Warm Up

    Forehand swing, backhand swing, contact, extension, finish. Get loose and feel the swing path.

  • 2

    Swing Snap

    Full swing stopping after contact. Hands should be above the barrel - not rolling over.

  • 3

    Stride Stop

    Load & stride then stop. Swing straight forward through the ball (no legs - as sudden as possible through ball).

  • 4

    Rocker Step

    3-count rocker step: back step, front step, back step, EXPLODE. Builds timing and rhythm.

  • 5

    Ortiz Drill

    Big leg kick back, knob to knee, balance and hold, separate (feel coil in hip) and explode. Do not fall forward - controlled landing.

  • 6

    Slow Gather

    Start open, gather together (emphasize the coil) and explode through the ball.

5 Hitting Approach

Be on the "Offensive" & Be "Aggressive"

Go up to bat looking to swing. Be aggressive and anticipate your pitch.

Attack every pitch like you are going to swing. The challenge is knowing when to shut it off if it's not your pitch.

Fundamentals

Tall & Strong Stance

Hands/bat should be behind head, above the letters of your uniform. Feet slightly wider than shoulders with a little bend and bounce in your legs. Stance should be comfortable and balanced.

Don't Disconnect

Do not disconnect and drop the hands. Drive your hands through the zone and "swing the box" - keeping your swing compact and powerful.

Tiny Load, Quick Explode

Your loading step should be a small shift initiator backwards, leading to a coiling of the back hip. Do not lean back - stay balanced.

Trigger the Swing

When you stride, your front foot hitting the ground initiates an explosion in your hips and core, triggering your swing on every single pitch.

Stay Compact & Tight

Keep everything controlled. Your stride should not be longer than 1 bat length. A compact swing is a quick swing.

Head & Eyes

Focus on the pitcher and ball. Follow the ball through the zone and watch your barrel make contact. Chin goes from front shoulder to back shoulder during swing. Do not pull your head!

Practice Drills

Tee Work

Set tee at different heights and locations. Focus on driving through the ball with proper mechanics. 25-50 swings per session.

Soft Toss

Partner tosses balls from the side at 45-degree angle. Work on timing and hand-eye coordination. Vary locations.

Dry Swings

Practice swing mechanics without a ball. Use a mirror to check form. Focus on balance and rotation. Great for muscle memory.

Front Toss

Coach/parent tosses from a screen in front. Simulates live pitching timing. Focus on pitch recognition and contact.

Pro Tips for Better Hitting

  • Keep your head still and eyes on the ball from pitcher's hand to bat contact
  • Stay balanced - if you're falling forward or backward, you'll lose power
  • Grip the bat in your fingers, not your palms - this allows better bat control
  • Think "hit it where it's pitched" - inside pitches go to pull side, outside to opposite field
