1) Cone Passing

Cone spacing can vary for age and should be all over ½ field.
3 groups (red/blue/yellow), balls to about 1/3 of the players.

Player A performs a skill then passes to player B: red to blue to yellow to red etc.
Player A then moves to an open cone. Player B performs skill and passes to another player.

  • Sets last 2-3 minutes, 1 minute between sets
  • 5 sets of 3 minutes
  • Cones about 15 yards apart

Skill varies (10 quick touches, 3 rollovers, scissors, etc.) each set

Passing varies (short pass, long pass, give/go, chip, etc.) each set

2) Ball Fakes

Cones 10-15 yards apart in straight line.
Groups of 4-6 players, 2-3 players at each end of the line.

Player A attacks middle cone and performs ball fake. After last touch, passes to B.
B then attacks middle cone, performs fake, passes to player C.

  • Sets of 2-3 minutes, with 1 minute between sets
  • Ball fakes vary (scissor, shot fake, etc.) each set
  • For younger players, could be a simple outside or inside foot cut
  • Work both feet

3) Technical Possession Activity

Make 2 squares with a small goal at one end about 8-15 yards apart.
Each team starts in their own square.

Team A gets the ball and 2-3 defenders from Team B try to defend.
After a certain number of passes, Team A may try to score.
If Team B wins the ball, they pass to their square.
Team A then sends the same number of defenders.

  • Passes, touches, square size, number of defenders vary based upon skill and age
  • To increase competitiveness, keep score

4) Big 1v1 Box

15x20 yards. 4 "goals" about 1 yard inside box on the long side.
Place pinnies down as goals.

4 lines of players behind each of the goals.
Player A makes long diagonal pass to B.
Player C does the same to D.

B and D attack either goal.
A and C defend.

If A or C steals ball, they attack opposite goals, while B or D must recover.
Players score by dribbling over the pinnie.

  • Vary box size for age
  • Can progress to 2v2

Emphasis: Defenders must sprint to cut off close goal; quick attack and counterattack; correct defensive stance

5) Team Transition Activity

3-4 teams of 4-7 players.

Place cones across the top of the 18-yard box (teams on defense, by the goal). GK/coach plays the ball to Team B, which remains on the first set of cones at the 18-yard line.

Team A sprints out to defend, while Team B quickly attacks to try to score before turnover.
Team A gets behind the 18-yard cones.
Team C moves up.
GK/coach plays the ball to Team C, as Team B becomes defense, and Team C attacks.

  • Move cones back to give more time, move cones forward to increase difficulty

Emphasis: Few touches to increase speed of play; quickly get in defensive position

6) 2v2 to 2v1

Full sized goals about 30 yards apart.
Penalty box width.

GK/coach plays ball to A or B.
Both players attack, while C and D defend.

Attack ends on shot or turnover.
Whoever touches it last (A or B) then gets back on defense, while C and D counterattack.
The counterattack starts on steal or quick inbound from GK/coach.

Emphasis on offense: Quick attack; quick decisions; pass only when needed

Emphasis on defense: Stop ball; force outside; help defender
